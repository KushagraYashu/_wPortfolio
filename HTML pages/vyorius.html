Key Points

At Vyorius, I was appointed as a Unity Developer and was responsible for developing and maintaining their “Drone Flight Simulator”, “Drone Test Simulator”, and “Drone Soccer” products. The products had basic features implemented by the team beforehand. The major tasks for me were to add new features or levels based on client requests and also to fix any bugs and fill any production gaps.

Training simulator tasks:
Firstly, I was assigned to develop a new level, Level 6, “The Lighthouse”. At that level, I created a whole terrain with the predetermined lighthouse challenges designed to train the roll with the ascent feature.

I also worked on the rejuvenation of other levels. I also had to devise a new technique for the camera movement for the drone camera and achieved it with the “Cinemachine” component provided by the Unity Engine.

Apart from that, I was assigned to develop a logic for the athletes at the stadium level. The athletes have to run circularly. I achieved the same with the help of the “sin” and “cos” wave functionality of the C# language.

I also worked on the environment upgrade, maintaining the quality of the assets or setting up animations according to need and quality, adding the required props and level designing, and managing the appropriate audio such as flight sounds or crash sounds.

Test simulator tasks:
I was assigned to develop 4 patterns (rectangle, circle, infinity, and helix) based on the standard measurements and deploy them on the web (Vyorius Simulator Website) and Windows systems (.exe builds).

I implemented key logic for the scoring system, the scoring is very tight and based on the regulations. The logic is to find the number of times the player deviated from the correct path and the amount of deviation, the score is then calculated based on that information.

I also set up the environment for the Test Sim. The environment includes a city background with some characters overlooking the test.
